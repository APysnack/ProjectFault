About Me

Hi! My name is Alan. I was born in St. Petersburg, Florida with Bilaterial Microtia and Atresia. Because of this, I am mostly deaf. Around the age of 12, I began writing songs and experimenting with music production, as well as utilizing Adobe Photoshop to create basic graphic designs.

After turning 21, I moved to Maryland where I performed Stand Up Comedy at a number of open mics and competitions. It was in Maryland that I met my wife, Brooke, who has been the best part of my life for over {{ anniversary_years }} years.

At 29, I made the decision to return to college and completed my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science with a 4.0 GPA. I now work as a software engineer and couldn't imagine doing anything else. I've accumulated many hobbies and interests over the years, software development included, and hope to accumulate more as I get older. I have a strong passion for learning as much as I can and trying to create new things.

About This Website

Project Fault is a full-stack web application designed to consolidate the many creative projects I've worked on over the years. Moreover, the creation and deployment of this web application were part of my personal journey to develop a better understanding of web development and cloud computing. The client-side interface was developed using HTML5, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript. The back-end uses Python, Flask, and SQL Alchemy to implement a MySQL database. The site in its original form was deployed via 2 Linode servers, but I have since migrated to AWS and implemented a very basic pipeline for CI/CD.
